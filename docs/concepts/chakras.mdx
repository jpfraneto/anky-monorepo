# The 8 Chakras

Each chakra represents a fundamental dimension of human experience. When you write an anky, the system reads the energetic signature of your words and maps them to one of these eight centers.

## Chakra Map

| # | Chakra | Theme | Kingdom | Color |
|---|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Root (Muladhara) | Survival, grounding, belonging | Primordia | Red |
| 2 | Sacral (Svadhisthana) | Creativity, desire, emotion | Emblazion | Orange |
| 3 | Solar Plexus (Manipura) | Power, will, identity | Chryseos | Yellow |
| 4 | Heart (Anahata) | Love, connection, grief | Eleasis | Green |
| 5 | Throat (Vishuddha) | Expression, truth, voice | Voxlumis | Blue |
| 6 | Third Eye (Ajna) | Intuition, insight, vision | Insightia | Indigo |
| 7 | Crown (Sahasrara) | Transcendence, unity, spirit | Claridium | Violet |
| 8 | Soul Star (Vyapini) | Integration, wholeness, mystery | Poiesis | White/Gold |

## How Chakra Detection Works

The chakra resonance is determined by Claude analyzing the full text of your anky. This is not keyword matching — it's semantic analysis of the emotional and thematic content.

A writing about fear of losing your home maps to Root. A writing about not being able to say what you mean maps to Throat. A writing about the death of a parent might map to Heart or Crown depending on how you processed it.

The system returns a number 1-8. That number determines:
- Which **kingdom** your cuentacuentos is set in
- Which **city** within that kingdom
- Which **characters** populate the story
- What **landscape** the images depict

## Why 8?

The traditional chakra system has 7. Anky adds an 8th — the Soul Star — because integration matters. The 8th chakra is where all the others converge. It maps to the kingdom of Poiesis (the same name as the server), which represents the act of creation itself.

Writing that doesn't fit neatly into survival, creativity, power, love, expression, intuition, or transcendence — writing that is about the strange experience of being alive and making things — lands here.
